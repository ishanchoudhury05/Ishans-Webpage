Library

Reading is one of my past times. I don't particularly read a wide variety of books, but I have thoroughly enjoyed what I have read. I typically use my 5th-generation kindle and occasionally a physical book

Many books overlap in themes, but I shall try my best to organise each book into loose topics. I will write a brief description of each book

Currently Reading

  • ICO: Castle in the Mist - Miyuki Miyabe: An adaptation of the video game "Ico" for the PlayStation 2. It expands upon the story and world in the perspective of the author
  • The Metaphysics of Morals - Immanuel Kant: A comprehensive framework for what Kant understands as morality
  • Lolita - Vladimir Nabokov: A controversial novel covering taboo themes of a relationship between a man and child. Despite this, it is held in high regards for its literature

Philosophy

  • The Art of Living a Meaningless Existence - Robert Pantano: What is the meaning of life? Pantano attempts to explain this question in the perspective of a variety of philosophical ideologies
  • The Book of Five Rings - Miyamoto Musashi: Initially a book meant to guide in martial strategy, but it also includes philosophical ideas and principles of personal conduct
  • The Daily Stoic - Ryan Holiday & Stephen Hanselman: Organised into 366 entries and meant to be read one per day, this book breaks down quotes from stoic philosophers and apply them to modern life
  • Stoicism for Inner Peace - Einzelganger: Leaning heavily into self-help, this book discusses stoicism as practical advice for a modern audience

Self-Help

  • Atomic Habits - James Clear: A scientific and psychological understanding to habits and how to master them
  • Can't Hurt Me - David Goggins: A former Navy SEAL, ultramarathon runner, and endurance athlete detailing his struggles in this autobiographical form
  • Digital Minimalism - Cal Newport: Exploring the concept of digital minimalism and focusing on what really matters by removing what doesn't
  • Feel the Fear and Do It Anyway - Susan Jeffers: Discussing the common human experience of fear and practical strategies to overcome them
  • Stolen Focus - Johann Hari: Exploring a modern crisis of attention (or lack of) and discussing a variety of reasons that may cause it
  • The Obstacle Is the Way - Ryan Holiday: Drawing inspiration from stoicism, this book discusses the importance of mindset when facing obstacles in our lives
  • Discipline is Destiny - Ryan Holiday: Linking discipline and personal success with many living/lived examples

Fiction

  • 1984 - George Orwell: A dystopian novel set in a totalitarian future that discusses themes of government oppression, surveillance, propaganda, and individual freedom
  • Reboot - Amy Tintera: A dystopian future where a deadly virus kills millions of people. However, select dead individuals begin to "reboot", coming back to life as an enhanced being
  • Rebel - Amy Tintera: The end to the "reboot" duology. A continuation of Wren's and Callum's story
  • Metro 2033 - Dmitry Glukhovsky: A post-apocalyptic science fiction novel about the survival of individuals who reside in the underground train network of moscow post WWIII

Books I Would Like To Read

  • Beyond Good and Evil - Friedrich Nietzche: Arguably Nietzsche's most influential work, challenging conventional morality and critiquing traditional philosophical ideas, with proposals for new
  • Deep Work - Cal Newport: Discussion on the topic of focusing without distraction on cognitively demanding tasks
  • Meditations - Marcus Aurelius: A collection of personal writings by the Roman Emperor who ruled from 161AD to 180AD. These writings were never intended to for publication
  • What We Owe to Each Other - T.M. Scanlon: A book exploring the fundamental questions in moral philosophy, focusing on moral obligations and the principles that govern our interactions within a society
  • Why Does Inequality Matter - T.M. Scanlon: A discussion about economic inequality and an exploration in why it is a matter of moral concern
  • How to Make the Best Coffee at Home - James Hoffmann: World Barista Champion teaching how to make quality barista-level drink at home
